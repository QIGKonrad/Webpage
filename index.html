Welcome

Welcome to the Quantum Information Group. This group is dedicated to the research in the field of quantum information and optics. We are part of the School of Chemistry and Physics at the University of KwaZulu-Natal.

Quantum information is the information stored in the state of quantum systems such as photons, atoms and molecules. Quantum information does not obey the rules of classical information theory. For example it cannot be copied. This leads to the possibility to securely transmit data - safe against eavesdropping (quantum cryptography). Another application of quantum information processing is quantum computers, which are a new type of computer being able to solve certain tasks much faster than ordinary computers.

In particular we are investigating the following problems:

  • Measurement and control of single quantum system
  • Efficient production and detection of single photons.
  • Quantum Cryptography with weak measurements.
  • Reduction of entanglement in noisy quantum channels.
